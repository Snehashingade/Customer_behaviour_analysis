# Customer Shopping Behaviour Analysis

An end-to-end data analytics project analysing customer shopping patterns
across 3,900 transactions using Python, PostgreSQL, and Power BI.

## Project Overview
This project uncovers insights into spending patterns, customer segments,
product preferences, and subscription behaviour to support strategic
business decisions.

## Tools & Technologies
- **Python** (pandas) — Data cleaning & EDA
- **PostgreSQL** — Business query analysis
- **Power BI** — Interactive dashboard

## Project Structure
- `notebooks/` — Jupyter notebook with full EDA
- `sql/` — PostgreSQL queries for 10 business questions
- `reports/` — Analysis report PDF
- `dashboard/` — Power BI dashboard file
- `presentation/` — Project presentation
- `data/` — Raw dataset

## Key Analyses
1. Revenue by Gender
2. High-Spending Discount Users
3. Top 5 Products by Rating
4. Shipping Type Comparison
5. Subscriber vs. Non-Subscriber Revenue
6. Discount-Dependent Products
7. Customer Segmentation (New / Returning / Loyal)
8. Top 3 Products per Category
9. Repeat Buyers & Subscription Likelihood
10. Revenue by Age Group

## 📊 Key Findings
- Male customers generated 2x more revenue (£157,890 vs £75,191)
- 73% of customers are non-subscribers — a major growth opportunity
- Young Adults are the highest revenue-generating age group ($62,143)
- Hat, Sneakers & Coat are the most discount-dependent products
- 3,116 out of 3,900 customers fall in the "Loyal" segment

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Boost subscription sign-ups with exclusive member benefits
- Reward loyal customers to improve retention
- Review discount policy to protect profit margins
- Target Young Adults and Express Shipping users in campaigns

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — Shopping Behaviour Dataset
- Rows: 3,900 | Columns: 18
